Journeys and the Users.

Talent manager is searching for talents who fit the campaign. They create a list, add selected talents to it, reach out to them with an offer and settle on a budget.
Then they create a report and share it with the Client who reviews the talents and the budget, and submits the approved list.
Activation manager then connects with the selected influencers, uploads the content they created, shares it with the Client and once approved, launches the campaign.

Research Time.

We already had a list of requests from talent managers, but first we wanted to make sure we understand how they use the platform. I created a prototype with a bunch of tasks, and with the help of product managers we ran a user testing.

Multi-Faceted Search.

From the research we learned that each talent manager had a very different technique of searching and filtering. We ended up organizing the most frequently used filters on the top, and removed all those that were never used.
Talents and audiences are powered by separate filters. They can be searched by custom keywords or predefined tags.

Client Review.

Clients receive a public link with shortlist of talents. They are able to approve and rank the ones they like the most, and reject the ones they don’t, from their phones.
They can also easily communicate with the talent managers through comments.
