AI-Assisted Product Design Workflow

Managing Memberships at Scale

Designing a scalable memberships platform with AI integrated from discovery to production.

My role

  • Product Design
  • UX Strategy
  • Design Systems
  • Frontend Collaboration
  • AI Workflow Integration
  • QA & Production Validation

Focus

Using AI as a systems and acceleration layer from research through production validation.

Overview

A faster way to design without lowering the bar.

A growing digital platform needed to redesign and scale its memberships experience across web and mobile. The existing system had fragmented flows, inconsistent UI patterns, and increasing friction between product, design, and engineering teams.

The goal was not just to redesign screens. It was to create a production-ready system that could scale quickly while reducing iteration time across the entire product lifecycle.

My role combined product design, system thinking, frontend collaboration, and AI-assisted workflows.

Challenge

Membership flows were moving faster than the system around them.

The team faced three major problems:

  • Membership flows evolved faster than the design system.
  • Engineers repeatedly rebuilt similar UI patterns.
  • Product decisions lacked visibility across user feedback, edge cases, and implementation constraints.

Traditional design workflows were too linear for the speed required.

How do we design faster without sacrificing production quality?

Approach

AI became a systems layer, not a screen generator.

Instead of treating AI as a screen generator, I integrated it throughout the workflow as a systems and acceleration layer.

AI helped reduce repetitive work, surface edge cases faster, and speed up exploration, while human judgment remained critical for product thinking, prioritization, and production quality.

Workflow
01

Research & Pattern Analysis

I used AI to synthesize large volumes of stakeholder notes, support tickets, competitor patterns, usability feedback, and internal product documentation.

This helped identify recurring friction points across onboarding, billing, and account management flows.

AI Contribution

  • Clustered recurring UX complaints.
  • Summarized qualitative feedback.
  • Identified missing states and flow inconsistencies.
  • Generated alternative IA structures.

Human Layer

The outputs still required validation and prioritization. Product relevance, business tradeoffs, and UX clarity required human decisions.

02

Rapid Exploration

Instead of manually creating every direction from scratch, AI accelerated early exploration across flows, navigation structures, UX copy, component combinations, and edge-case scenarios.

AI Contribution

  • Generated alternative user flows.
  • Tested navigation structures.
  • Rewrote UX copy variations.
  • Prototyped edge-case scenarios.

Result

The team reviewed broader solution spaces in days instead of weeks.

03

Design System Integration

The project required scalable UI patterns across multiple membership states and permissions.

AI Contribution

  • Mapped inconsistent components.
  • Detected duplicate variants.
  • Suggested token structures.
  • Generated documentation drafts.

Human Layer

I refined hierarchy, interaction behavior, responsive logic, accessibility decisions, and component scalability.

04

Frontend Collaboration

One of the biggest workflow improvements came from reducing the gap between design and engineering.

AI Contribution

  • Generated frontend scaffolding.
  • Validated responsive behavior.
  • Checked implementation consistency.
  • Compared production UI against specs.

Review Shift

From “Does this match Figma?” to “Does this solve the product problem correctly?”

05

QA & Production Validation

As AI-generated outputs became more common, quality control became increasingly important. A major part of my role became acting as a constraint and validation layer.

AI Contribution

  • Identified missing edge cases.
  • Stress-tested flows.
  • Simulated inconsistent states.
  • Audited design-system usage.

Lesson

AI increases output speed. Systems thinking determines output quality.

Key Takeaway

AI changed where product design creates value.

AI did not replace product design. The leverage shifted from manually producing screens to defining systems, shaping workflows, validating quality, and orchestrating decisions across tools, teams, and outputs.

The future advantage is not prompting faster. It is building workflows that turn AI output into production-ready products.

Outcome

01Faster design iteration cycles.
02Improved design-engineering alignment.
03More scalable component architecture.
